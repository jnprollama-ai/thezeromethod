# Zero Marketing Agency Principles Checklist

**Our Commitment: Honest, No-Bullshit Products**

This checklist ensures every decision aligns with our core values. If something doesn't pass this test, we don't do it.

---

## Product Integrity

- [x] **We sell what we say we sell**
  - No phantom features
  - No vaporware promises
  - No "coming soon" placeholders in sales copy

- [x] **Pricing reflects actual value delivered**
  - $19 for 25 prompts + 10 templates = fair
  - No hidden upsells in checkout flow
  - No "enterprise" bait-and-switch tactics

- [x] **Copy matches reality**
  - "Save 5 hours" not "save 10+ hours"
  - Actual testimonials only (none yet = no testimonial section)
  - Specific, measurable claims only

## Marketing Honesty

- [x] **No false urgency**
  - No fake countdown timers
  - No "limited time" offers without actual limits
  - No scarcity tactics for digital products

- [x] **Transparent process**
  - We explain how prompts work
  - We admit limitations upfront
  - We don't oversell AI capabilities

- [x] **No misleading visuals**
  - Screenshots = real, not mockups
  - Before/after examples = actual customer results (none yet)
  - No stock photos pretending to be our customers

## Customer Experience

- [x] **Easy refund policy**
  - 30-day window clearly stated
  - No hoops to jump through
  - No "case by case" denials

- [x] **Real support**
  - Not "support within 24 hours" (we're faster)
  - Personal responses, not template answers
  - Honest about what we can/can't fix

## Development Process

- [x] **Iterate publicly**
  - Share progress updates truthfully
  - Admit when we break things
  - Explain why we make changes

- [x] **Quality over speed**
  - Better to ship later with confidence
  - No "good enough for now" compromises
  - Every feature must stand alone or be cut

---

**Daily Self-Check:**
Before end of each workday, answer:
1. Did everything shipped today pass this checklist? (Yes - blog draft aligns with our principles)
2. Is our website copy still 100% truthful? (Yes - audited today)
3. Would a customer be surprised by anything post-purchase? (No - we're selling exactly what we say)

If any answer is "no", we fix it before next shipment.
