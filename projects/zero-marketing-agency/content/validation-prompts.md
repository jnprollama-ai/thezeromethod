## Prompt #1: The Weekly Report Generator

**Use Case:** Marketing managers drowning in weekly reporting

**The Prompt:**
```
"I need to create a weekly marketing performance report. Here's my data:

[ paste your raw metrics here - traffic, conversions, spend, etc. ]

Please:
1. Analyze the trends and highlight 3 key insights
2. Identify 2 areas of concern with specific recommendations
3. Write an executive summary (3 bullet points) for my boss
4. Suggest 2 experiments for next week based on the data

Format as a professional report with headers."
```

**Why it works:** Saves 45-60 minutes of analysis and writing

**Reddit Title:** "I used this prompt to automate my weekly marketing reports. Saves me an hour every Friday."

**LinkedIn Angle:** "The prompt that got me 45 minutes back every week"

## Prompt #2: The Client Email Refiner

**Use Case:** Freelancers/consultants sending professional client communications

**The Prompt:**
```
"I need to send a professional email to a client. Here's what I want to say:

[ paste your rough draft or bullet points ]

Context:
- Relationship: [new client / ongoing / about to close]
- Tone needed: [formal / friendly but professional / urgent]
- Goal: [get approval / deliver update / handle a problem]

Please rewrite this as a polished, professional email that:
- Opens with appropriate context
- Is clear and concise
- Has a clear next step/call to action
- Maintains the right tone"
```

**Why it works:** Removes anxiety from difficult client conversations

**Reddit Title:** "This prompt has saved me from sending embarrassing client emails 20+ times"

**LinkedIn Angle:** "How to stop overthinking client emails"

## Prompt #3: The Meeting Summarizer

**Use Case:** Busy professionals with back-to-back meetings

**The Prompt:**
```
"I just finished a meeting. Here's what was discussed:

[ paste messy notes, transcript, or bullet points ]

Please create:
1. A one-sentence summary of the main outcome
2. A bulleted list of decisions made
3. A table with: Action Item | Owner | Due Date
4. A 2-sentence follow-up email I can send to confirm next steps

Be concise. I need this in under 30 seconds of reading."
```

**Why it works:** Turns meeting chaos into clear action items

**Reddit Title:** "Turn meeting notes into action items instantly with this prompt"

**LinkedIn Angle:** "How I stopped forgetting what happens in meetings"

## Prompt #4: The Proposal Framework

**Use Case:** Freelancers/consultants writing proposals

**The Prompt:**
```
"I'm writing a proposal for a [type of project] project. Here's what I know:

Client: [industry, size, pain points]
Project scope: [brief description]
My deliverables: [what you'll provide]
Timeline: [duration]
My rate: [hourly/project/retainer]

Please write a proposal that includes:
1. An opening that shows I understand their problem
2. A clear scope of work section
3. 3 options (Good/Better/Best pricing tiers)
4. A simple next step to move forward
5. A closing that builds confidence

Keep it professional but not stuffy. One page max."
```

**Why it works:** Removes blank-page syndrome, structures thinking

**Reddit Title:** "I used ChatGPT to write proposals that close. Here's the exact prompt."

**LinkedIn Angle:** "The proposal framework that helped me land $X in new business"

## Prompt #5: The Difficult Conversation Coach

**Use Case:** Managers having tough conversations (performance, scope creep, deadlines)

**The Prompt:**
```
"I need to have a difficult conversation with [person] about [topic].

The situation: [describe the issue neutrally]
My goal: [what do you want to achieve?]
The risks: [what could go wrong?]
My relationship with them: [describe dynamic]

Please:
1. Give me 3 ways to open this conversation
2. Write a script for the key points I need to make (2-3 paragraphs)
3. Suggest 2-3 ways to handle likely pushback
4. Recommend how to close the conversation positively

Be direct but empathetic. I want to preserve the relationship while addressing the issue."
```

**Why it works:** Reduces anxiety, provides structure for emotionally charged situations

**Reddit Title:** "How I stopped dreading difficult conversations at work"

**LinkedIn Angle:** "Leadership hack: using AI to prepare for tough talks"

## Posting Strategy
