## Template #1: Cold Outreach
**Use Case:** Reaching out to someone you don't know

```
Subject: [Specific value proposition] + [Their company]

Hi [Name],

I came across your [work/post/company] and [specific observation that shows you did your research].

[One sentence about who you are and why you're credible].

I'm reaching out because [specific reason - be clear about what's in it for them].

[Specific value proposition - what do they get?]

Worth a 15-minute conversation? I'm free [Day] or [Day] between [Time] and [Time].

If not, no worries - [easy out that keeps door open].

[Your name]
[Your title]
[One-line about your company if relevant]

P.S. [Optional: something personal that shows you know them]
```

## Template #2: Follow-Up After No Response
**Use Case:** Following up without being annoying

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [Name],

Quick follow-up on [brief reminder of what you sent].

I know you're busy, so to make this easy:

[Yes/No question they can answer in 10 seconds]?

If [yes]: [what happens next]
If [no]: [no hard feelings, keep the door open]

Either way, thanks for your time.

[Your name]
```

## Template #4: Introduction Request
**Use Case:** Asking for an introduction

```
Subject: Quick intro request

Hi [Name],

I'm looking to connect with [Target person/role] at [Company] because [specific reason].

I noticed you're connected on [LinkedIn/Twitter/etc.] - would you be comfortable making an introduction?

If yes, here's a brief blurb you can use:
"[Your name] helps [what you do]. They're reaching out because [reason]. Thought you two should connect."

If not, no problem at all - I know these requests can be awkward.

Either way, thanks for considering!

[Your name]
```

## Template #5: Declining Politely
**Use Case:** Saying no without burning bridges

```
Subject: Re: [Their subject]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for [reaching out / the invitation / the opportunity].

After [considering/looking at my schedule/reviewing priorities], I have to pass on this one.

[Optional brief reason: "My plate's full with [specific project]" or "Not the right fit for where I'm focused right now"]

[Alternative if applicable: "However, [Name] might be a great fit - want an intro?"]

Hope we can [collaborate/connect] down the road.

[Your name]
```

## Customization Guide

### Brackets Explained:
- **[Name]** = Recipient's name
- **[Specific value]** = Details unique to your situation
- **[Optional]** = Can remove if not applicable

### Tone Adjustments:

**More formal:**
- Use full sentences
- Avoid contractions
- Include titles

**More casual:**
- Shorter sentences
- Contractions okay
- Can remove formal sign-offs

### Pro Tips:

1. **Subject lines:** Specific > clever. "Q about your pricing page" beats "Quick question"
2. **Length:** Shorter = better. Aim for 100 words or less
3. **One ask:** Each email should have ONE clear purpose
4. **Easy out:** Always give them permission to say no
5. **Proofread:** Grammar mistakes kill credibility
