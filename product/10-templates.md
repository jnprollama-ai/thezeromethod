# 10 Professional Email Templates

## Template 1: Cold Outreach
**Subject:** Quick question about [their company/situation]

Hi [Name],

I came across [their company/project/work] and was impressed by [specific detail].

I help [target audience] [achieve specific outcome]. Working with [similar companies/recent clients], we typically see [specific result].

Would you be open to a brief 15-minute call next week to explore if this might be relevant for [their company]?

Either way, keep up the great work with [specific detail].

Best,
[Your Name]

---

## Template 2: Follow-Up After No Response
**Subject:** Following up: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

I wanted to circle back on my email from [timeframe] about [topic].

I understand you're busy. To be respectful of your time:

If this isn't a priority right now, I completely understand—just let me know and I won't follow up again.

If it is, I'm happy to [offer value—send specific info, schedule brief call, etc.] on [day] or [day].

What works best?

Thanks,
[Your Name]

P.S. [Add something relevant you noticed about their company—recent news, social post, etc.]

## Template 3: Professional "No"
**Subject:** Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for thinking of me for [opportunity/request]. I appreciate you considering me.

After careful consideration, I'm not going to be able to [accept/participate/do this] at this time. [Optional: Brief reason—"my schedule is currently full" or "this doesn't align with my current priorities"].

However, I'd be happy to [alternative if applicable—"connect you with someone who might be a better fit" or "revisit this in [timeframe]"].

I hope [project/event/initiative] goes well. Please keep me posted on how it turns out.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

## Usage Instructions

### Customization Tips:
1. **Personalize**: Always add specific details about the recipient
2. **Keep it brief**: Edit down if the email feels too long
3. **Match your tone**: Adjust formality based on your relationship
4. **Subject lines**: Test different subjects for better open rates
5. **Follow up**: Most emails need 2-3 follow-ups to get a response

### Best Practices:
- **Send Tuesday-Thursday**: Best open rates
- **Morning (8-10 AM)**: Higher engagement
- **Mobile-friendly**: 60%+ of emails are opened on phones
- **Clear CTA**: One clear action per email
- **Proofread**: Always check before sending

### Storage:
Save these templates in your email client's template folder or a document you can easily access. Customize them once, then reuse.
