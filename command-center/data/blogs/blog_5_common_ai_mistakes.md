## Mistake #1: Being Too Vague with Prompts

**The Problem:**
You type "write a blog post" and get generic garbage.

**Why It Happens:**
AI doesn't know your audience, your voice, your goals, or your constraints.

**The Fix:**
Use this formula: **"I'm [WHO] writing for [AUDIENCE] about [TOPIC]. I need [FORMAT] that's [TONE]. Include [SPECIFIC ELEMENTS]."

**Example:**
❌ "Write about marketing"
✅ "I'm a B2B SaaS founder writing for marketing managers about email automation. I need a 500-word blog post that's professional but conversational. Include 3 actionable tips and a call-to-action at the end."

**Time Saved:** 30 minutes per piece of content

---

## Mistake #2: Accepting the First Output

**The Problem:**
You get one response and call it done.

**Why It Happens:**
You think AI should be perfect on the first try. It's not.

**The Fix:**
Treat AI output as a first draft. Iterate:

1. "Make it more [specific quality]"
2. "Add an example about [topic]"
3. "Change the tone to be more [adjective]"
4. "Shorten this section"

**Pro Tip:** The magic happens in revision #2-4, not #1.

**Quality Improvement:** 3x better results

---

## Mistake #3: Not Fact-Checking

**The Problem:**
You copy-paste AI output without verifying facts.

**Why It Happens:**
AI sounds confident even when it's wrong (hallucination).

**The Fix:**
Always verify:
- Statistics and data
- Quotes and attributions
- Dates and timelines
- Technical specifications

**Red Flag:** If AI cites a specific study or statistic, Google it.

**Risk Avoided:** Embarrassing mistakes, misinformation, credibility loss

---

## Mistake #4: Forgetting the Context

**The Problem:**
You treat each prompt as isolated. No memory, no context.

**Why It Happens:**
You don't realize AI can reference previous parts of the conversation.

**The Fix:**
Use phrases like:
- "Based on what we discussed earlier..."
- "Referring to the outline you just created..."
- "Building on that idea..."

**Pro Tip:** Start major projects with: "I'm going to share a [document/project] with you. Remember this context for our conversation."

**Efficiency Gain:** 40% fewer repeated explanations

---

## Mistake #5: Using AI for the Wrong Tasks

**The Problem:**
You're using AI for tasks where it adds no value.

**Why It Happens:**
FOMO. Everyone says "use AI" so you try to force it everywhere.

**AI Is Great For:**
✅ First drafts
✅ Brainstorming
✅ Summarizing
✅ Editing and improving
✅ Repetitive formatting
✅ Research (with verification)

**AI Is Bad For:**
❌ Final creative decisions
❌ Tasks requiring deep expertise
❌ Anything requiring emotional intelligence
❌ Legal or medical advice
❌ Your unique voice and perspective

**The Rule:** Use AI for speed. Use humans for judgment.

## The Fix-It Checklist

Before using AI, ask yourself:

- [ ] Do I have a clear, specific prompt?
- [ ] Have I provided context?
- [ ] Will I iterate on the first output?
- [ ] Can I verify any facts independently?
- [ ] Is this the right tool for this task?
